The UpForge course catalog is your gateway to every learning path on the platform. Each course is built around real projects and structured video lessons, so you build practical skills from day one. Browse at your own pace, preview course details before committing, and enroll in as many courses as you like — there are no paywalls or enrollment limits.

## Navigating the Catalog

Open the course catalog by clicking **Courses** in the left sidebar. UpForge loads every available course and displays them in a responsive grid — four columns on large screens, two on tablets, and a single column on mobile.

The catalog shows all courses immediately when the page loads. As you type in the search bar, the grid filters in real time — no page reload needed.

## Searching for Courses

A search bar sits at the top of the catalog page. Enter any keyword — a topic, skill, or phrase — and UpForge instantly filters the grid to courses whose **title** or **description** matches your query.

If no courses match your search, UpForge shows a "No courses found" message with a **Clear Search** button so you can reset and browse the full catalog again.

## What Each Course Card Shows

Every card in the catalog gives you enough detail to decide whether a course is right for you before you enroll.

| Field | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Thumbnail** | A preview image for the course |
| **Title** | The name of the course |
| **Description** | A short summary of what the course covers |
| **Duration** | Total estimated watch time in minutes |
| **XP value** | The total experience points you earn for completing the course |
| **Total lessons** | How many video lessons make up the course |
| **Progress bar** | Appears only on courses you are enrolled in; shows the percentage of lessons you have completed |

## Course Difficulty Levels

Every course carries a difficulty label — **Beginner**, **Intermediate**, or **Advanced** — so you can match a course to your current skill level. Courses default to **Beginner** unless otherwise specified, making the catalog a safe starting point even if you are brand new to a topic.

## Finding the Right Course

Before you enroll, read the course description carefully. It summarises the skills the course targets, the projects you will build, and what you should already know. If the description mentions tools or concepts you are not yet familiar with, look for a prerequisite course in the catalog first.

You can also look at the XP value as a rough signal of course depth — a higher point value typically reflects more lessons and broader coverage.

<Tip>
  All UpForge courses are sourced from curated YouTube playlists. Every lesson is a hand-picked YouTube video embedded directly in the player, so you always get high-quality, up-to-date content without leaving the platform.
</Tip>
